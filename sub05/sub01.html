Local Partnerships

Local partnerships benefit both parties. Location-based programs provide students with invaluable experience and insight, whereas partners benefit from input and contributions from students to their pivotal initiatives.
Partners collaborate with NCS to develop programs, donating substantial amounts of time and resources, looking forward to worthwhile exchanges with students.

Traditional methods of teaching are limited to conventional in-class lectures and homework.
However, NCS challenges students to a non-stop learning process where they actively engage with the world surrounding them.
We believe that rigorous academics of students can be enhanced by experiential learning programs that make use of the diversity of each project site, adding both breadth and depth to a student’s intellectual development, capabilities, and overall character.

Regular activities with local individuals and organizations is a perfect opportunity to blend a student’s studies with their personal interests.
To introduce relative concepts of each term, NCS hosts a series of discussions, presentations, and tours, which will allow students to broaden their horizons.

The more structured co-curricular programs explore deeply into crucial topics and issues, focusing on practical learning. Through team activities and work sessions with partner organizations, these programs encourage students to understand the challenges facing each society and inspire them to examine a specific concern. These explorations incorporate concepts and materials from students’ courses to bring the lessons into existence.

Location-based assignments

Each term, students will be required to complete an assignment that extends their understanding of the concepts and skills covered in class into the urban context. This project requires students to exercise the fundamental skills at the core of the curriculum by applying them to new situations. Students contextualize their coursework, through research, case studies, and directed exercises. These required assignments are often connected to recurring activities that students can elect to undertake with one of our co-curricular partners. By providing a means for continued involvement with these organizations, students are able to gain a much deeper level of insight into the issues they are grappling with.

Challenge Projects

Each location presents new opportunities for working on a range of societal issues, from government, art, and culture to science, technology, and commerce. In collaboration with local partners, impact projects allow students to build upon — and inform — their formal studies by helping contribute to these important efforts. Because these projects involve long-term initiatives associated with real-world challenges, they often readily connect to a student’s end-of-term projects. In this way, impact projects are an excellent way for students to have a significant effect on society by practicing the skills and applying the concepts they have learned in classes.
