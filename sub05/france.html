Education

The French education system is similar to that of the US. It consists of Primary school, collège (middle school), and lycée (high school). In the final year of their lycée, students take the baccalauréat (bac) examination to obtain the baccalauréat diploma.

Culture

France has always been the center of Western cultural development for centuries. It is still considered as one of the most culture-rich countries. France holds 45 sites in the UNESCO World Heritage List, 1200 museums, and 85 national historical monuments.

Art
French Port of le havre by Claude Monet

France is home to numerous artists like Claude Monet, Henri Matisse, Auguste Rodin, and even foreign artists like the Spanish painter Pablo Picasso and Dutch painter Vincent van Gogh, who settled and worked in Paris. The extensive history of France allows it to boast a wide array of architecture, dating back to the Medieval ages and the French Renaissance.

Entertainment
Inventor of Cinema, Lumière Brothers

France also has a strong link with Cinema. The French brothers of Auguste and Louis, Lumière, are credited with creating cinematography in 1895. As such, France remains a leader in filmmaking. Even more, France is the only nation in the world where American films make up only 50% of total film revenue.

Cuisine
Baguette
Croissant

French cuisine is also a crucial part of the culture. It is one of the world’s most renowned cuisines, demanded by many international fans. The usual French meal consists of 3 parts: entreè (introductory dish), plat principal (main dish), fromage (cheese dish) or dessert. France is also known for its publication of the Michelin Guide, which bestows Michelin Stars to restaurants, serving as an indication of excellence.

Sports
French Soccer player, Zidane

France is no stranger to sporting events. In fact, it has been holding a handful of the largest annual sporting events such as the Tour de France, FIFA World Cups, Rugby World Cups, UEFA Euro, and even the Olympics. France offers public sports facilities such as football pitches, rugby stadiums, and athletic tracks that are free-to-use. Furthermore, its abundant landmass provides ample opportunity for outdoor sports and activities.
