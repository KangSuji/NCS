Climate, Geography

The Korean peninsula is surrounded by 3 seas, and consists of mountainous terrain where most of the land is not arable.
There are over 3000 islands that stretch across the western and southern coasts of Korea. South Korea has a generally humid continental climate along with humid subtropical climate.

Korea has a short period (called Jangma) of intense rainfall that starts around the end of June and persists throughout July.
Korea is known to have all four seasons.

Education
South Korea’s education system is divided into three stages

Chodeung hakgyo (elementary school), Jung hakgyo (middle school), Godeung hakgyo (high school).
Unlike the American educational system, South Korea’s elementary school consists of 6 years.
After the 6 years, students move on to middle school, where they will spend 3 years studying.
The Korean high school only consists of 3 years and students will take the CSAT (College Scholastic Ability Test), Suneung, which is regarded as the university entrance exam administered by the Ministry of Education.

Culture

South Korea is rich in culture, with 21 UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritages of Humanity and 15 World Heritage Sites.
Due to its long years of conflict, much of the traditional architecture has been constructed and destroyed repeatedly.
This resulted in a mixture of different styles and designs that hint at its history.

Entertainment
BTS (Bangtan Sonyeondan)
Squid Game

Entertainment in South Korea is thriving across the world.
The cultural phenomenon, known as Hallyu, has been spreading globally, placing South Korea as a major soft power in Asia.
South Korean music, known as K-pop, has developed into an international sensation, gathering attention from all over the world. Recently, BTS (Bangtan Sonyeondan) has been setting numerous world records, starting a new wave of Hallyu.
Furthermore, South Korean dramas and movies have begun gaining fame internationally.
Korean movies such as the Oscar-winning ‘Parasite’ and global sensations such as the Netflix Series ‘Squid Game’ have signaled a new era of Korean soft power.

Bulgogi

Cuisine in Korea is worth the visit. With the rise in popularity of Korean multimedia content, the demand for South Korean food products is also rising. For example, the recent Oscar-winning movie ‘Parasite’ is said to have resulted in a 29.2% jump in global sales of Korean instant noodles. A traditional Korean meal will consist of abundant side dishes (banchan), a main dish, a bowl of rice, and a bowl of soup.

Sports
Korean Soccer Player, Son Heung Min

Sports in Korea is dominated by football and baseball.
The country has hosted global sports events such as the Summer Olympics in 1988 and Winter Olympics in 2018, the FIFA World Cup in 2002, and multiple Asian Games.
Many public sports complexes can be found spread across the country, providing free access to its facilities.
eSports is becoming more popular for younger audiences in South Korea.
