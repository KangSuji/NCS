Climate, Geography

Australia is the world’s largest island and 6th largest country in the world, located in Oceania. Due to its enormous landmass, the geography of the country varies by region. The north-eastern parts of the country are filled with tropical rainforests, while the south-eastern, south-western, eastern parts of the country are mostly mountain ranges. The central region of Australia is a desert and makes up the largest proportion of the land. Climate in Australia, like its geography, is not uniform. The climate ranges from Mediterranean to Alpine throughout the country. Australia has 4 seasons that are opposite in terms of time from other nations in the northern hemisphere.

Education

School education in Australia is compulsory up to the age of 16 (year 10). The educational system is divided in to 3 stages of Primary School, Secondary School, and Senior Secondary School. Yet, it holds the record of the highest ratio of international students. Top universities in Australia are: Australian National University (ANU), University of Sydney, University of Melbourne, University of New South Wales (UNSW) and University of Queensland (UQ).

Culture

On top of the 16 natural World Heritage Sites, Australia is also known for its diverse culture that has been mostly due to its long history of immigration. Although it still has a primarily Western culture, influenced by European settlers, it has incorporated many elements from the waves of immigration from the rest of the world.

Media
Chris Hemsworth

The Australian Media experienced a boom that came in the silent film era, but was met with a cessation in film production in the 1960s. By the 1970s, the Australian New Wave of media, funded by the government, brought about successful films such as Gallipoli, Crocodile Dundee and Mad Max. Australia is known for its actors, having won more than 55 Academy Awards and 192 nominations.

Cuisine
Pavlova
Australian Meat Pie

Multiple cultures have affected Australia Cuisine. The British and Irish culture of Australia resulted in dishes such as the Australian Meat Pie. The rich number of Cantonese settlers produced one of Australia’s favorite snacks, Chiko Roll. Other iconic foods of Australia include: pavlova, lamington, meat pies, and vegemite.

Sports
Cricket in Australia

Cricket and football (rugby) are the two predominant sports in Australia. Cricket is considered to be the national sport of Australia, evident from the country's participation in every Cricket World Cup and tournaments. Australia is one of the 5 nations that have participated in every Summer Olympic Games in the modern era, hosting 2 of them in 1956 (Melbourne) and 2000 (Sydney). Australia also hosts the global events such as Australian Open Tennis Grand Slam Tournament and Formula One Grand Prix. The country is also known for its abundant opportunity for water-based sports and outdoor adventure.
