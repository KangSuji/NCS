Design Thinking Process

Design Thinking Process is Stanford University’s educational method for creative problem solving. Students at NCS work on projects by following the design thinking process.

For example, let’s look in to the project modeling example of Malaysia.
Empathize
Interview local Malaysian merchants looking into e-commerce solutions.
Define
Identify the challenge of local merchants: they remain isolated from the e-commerce market.
Explore
Brainstorm ideas to help local merchants.
Prototype
Design solution: online commerce website for local merchants to sell their products on.
Test
Design solution: online commerce website for local merchants to sell their products on.
Contribute
Present solution to the community and contribute to launching the website.
NC Capstone Program

NC Capstone Program is a four-year program that consists of four NCS courses. In these courses, students work on various projects that require them to apply knowledge and skills across multiple subject areas and encourage them to find innovative ways to solve real-world problems. This program equips students with 21st-century skills that are increasingly valued by colleges and contemporary workplaces and prepares them to become contributing members of global society.

Grade Course Titles
Grade 9 Introduction to Design Thinking
Grade 10 Global Citizenship Survey
Grade 11 AP Seminar: Cross-disciplinary Seminar
Grade 12 AP Research: Capstone Resarch

How does NCS cultivate Game Changers?

Project Based Learning
Explore

Explore the world and gain insight.
Learning about the world through textbooks and the Internet has its limits.
But Project-based Learning at NCS allows you to gain first-hand experience and global insight into the world,
accumulating knowledge of concepts and specifics.

Analyze

Analyze real-world needs.
After exploring the world, you will comprehend the knowledge that you have gained.
You will be able to understand the material or idea being communicated.
In this process, you will draw connections among ideas and real-world needs.

Create

Createyour own ______ .
Synthesize elements and parts of what you have analyzed.
After you have done so, use that abstraction and apply them to new situations.
You will produce new or original work that will serve as an embodiment of the abstraction that you have created. In accordance with our TAC’s method, the output of your project-based learning can be in any form.
Research papers, presentations, performances, original products, be creative

Communicate

Communicate your values.
Once you have created your own output, you must be able to explain ideas and concepts behind it.
Here, you will communicate the values you wish to convey through your output.

Reflect

Reflect on your experiences.
Reflect on what you have experienced throughout your journey.
Criticize your work and identify ways to improve. Make sure you remember what you have learned throughout the cycle.
Once you have achieved this level, return to the first stage of the cycle for a new project.
